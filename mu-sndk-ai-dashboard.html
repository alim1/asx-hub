Technology stack mapped to AI workloads

Workload / requirement
Training clusters
Need extreme memory bandwidth, low latency, local working-set fit, high power efficiency near GPUs.

Inference clusters
Mix of accelerator memory, DDR/LP memory, KV cache, and fast SSD tiers for offload/retrieval.

Mass storage / data lake
Need capacity, endurance, $/TB, rack density, and throughput per watt more than nanosecond-class latency.
Micron (MU)
HBM / DRAM / enterprise SSD

Best aligned to training and high-performance inference. HBM4 is shipping in volume, LP DRAM and DDR support inference architectures, and Micron also participates in data-center SSDs.

SanDisk (SNDK)
NAND / SSD / capacity flash

Best aligned to storage-heavy inference, vector databases, data lakes, and enterprise capacity tiers. Less exposed to accelerator-side scarcity, more exposed to storage scaling and SSD qualification cycles.

AI infrastructure requirements

Compute memoryMicron advantaged
Primary bottlenecks: bandwidth, latency, power, package integration, and supply trade ratio. HBM and advanced DRAM sit closest to AI accelerator economics.
Storage fabricSanDisk advantaged
Primary bottlenecks: capacity, endurance, throughput per watt, cost/TB, and qualification with hyperscalers/OEMs. Enterprise SSDs feed retrieval, checkpointing, vector DB, and archival tiers.
Inference shiftShared
As inference broadens, DRAM still matters for token economics and active memory, while NAND/SSD matters more for cache offload, model repositories, and data gravity.

Interpretation for 2026-2027

Micron
Higher-beta AI infrastructure name. Reward is largest if AI capex remains compute-first and HBM stays supply-constrained. Risk is that a shift from training buildout to storage/network/utilization optimization compresses premium memory economics before new capacity fully earns through.
SanDisk
More of a picks-and-shovels storage expression on AI. Reward improves if 2027 spending broadens from accelerators into retention, checkpointing, RAG, vector search, and large capacity SSD fleets. Risk sits in classic NAND cyclicality, JV execution, and slower-than-expected hyperscaler qualification ramps.
Portfolio framing
MU is the sharper instrument for AI training / premium compute memory; SNDK is the cleaner instrument for flash capacity growth and storage intensity. Together they cover opposite sides of the same AI infrastructure stack.
