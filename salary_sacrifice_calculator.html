Salary sacrifice reduces your assessable income before tax is calculated. The ATO taxes the sacrificed portion at 15% inside super — not at your marginal rate. LITO (Low Income Tax Offset) of up to $700 phases out above $37,500 and disappears at $66,667. Tax rates sourced from the ATO for FY2024–25.

⚠️ Concessional cap alert: Your total concessional contributions (employer SG + sacrifice) exceed the $30,000 cap for FY2024–25. Amounts above the cap are taxed at your marginal rate (less a 15% offset). Consider reducing your sacrifice.

⚠️ Division 293 tax: Your income + concessional contributions exceeds $250,000. An additional 15% tax applies to your concessional contributions (total 30%), reducing the super tax advantage.

Every $1,000 you sacrifice costs you $680 in take-home pay (because it avoids income tax + Medicare). Inside super it becomes $850 after the 15% contributions tax. The net difference — $170 — is money that stays working for you instead of going to the ATO. Total annual tax saving = sacrifice × (MTR + Medicare − 15%).

Sacrifice scenario: employer SG + sacrifice both enter super as concessional contributions and are taxed at 15% inside the fund. After-tax scenario: the same gross sacrifice amount is paid to you as salary, taxed at your marginal rate + Medicare (32%), then the net is contributed to super as non-concessional (no entry tax). Both scenarios grow at the same assumed return. The sacrifice advantage widens every year due to compounding on the initial tax saving. After-tax balance includes employer SG in both scenarios.
