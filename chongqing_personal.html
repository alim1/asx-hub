Why the itinerary is structured this way — the geographic logic behind each cluster.
Cluster A Wulong District — Days 5
Fairy Mountain (仙女山) and Three Natural Bridges are both in Wulong District, ~180km from the city. Combining them on the same overnight trip saves a 6-hr round trip. Fairy Mountain is in the morning (cooler, meadow in soft light). Three Bridges is afternoon (dramatic canyon light). Overnight in Fairy Mountain area means no rush.
Cluster B Pengshui County — Day 6 (continuation from Wulong)
Pengshui is ~98km south of Wulong (~2 hrs). Jiuli City, Wujiang Gallery Cruise, and Wujiang Riverfront are all within Pengshui. Continuing from Wulong is far more efficient than two separate 5-hr round trips from the city. Overnight in Pengshui, then train back next morning.
Drone show Saturday 14 June — locked in
The show runs every Saturday at 9pm. June 14 is the Saturday in your trip window. Day 4 is placed on June 14 specifically to catch this. Zoo in the morning is low-stress and nearby; Chaotianmen in the evening for the show.
City loop Days 1–4 — Jiefangbei base
Liziba, Ropeway, Three Gorges Museum, Ciqikou, Guanyinqiao, Starlight 68, and Zoo are all reachable within ~30–45 min by metro from a Jiefangbei hotel. No overnight required — efficient metro hops keep energy high.
Return day Day 7 — Dazu is on the way back
Dazu Rock Carvings are west of the city on the Chengdu rail corridor — 40 min by high-speed train. Returning from Pengshui via train means you pass through the western suburbs. Easy to add Dazu as a stop before returning to Jiefangbei. Skip if the family is tired — WFC Deck alone makes a fine final afternoon.

Pack extra for Fairy Mountain
At 1,900m, Fairy Mountain runs 10–12°C cooler than the city. Bring a light jacket even in June — mornings can be genuinely cold.
Wujiang cruise timing
The 3:30pm departure is ideal — afternoon light makes the jade-green water glow. Boat fills up fast in summer; arrive 30 min early to buy tickets.
Drone show position
Chaotianmen riverfront is the official launch zone — you can't miss it there. Jiangbeizui Park (across the Jialing River) gives a slightly more elevated vantage. Both are excellent.
WeChat Pay is essential
Pengshui food stalls and small vendors often don't accept foreign cards. Load WeChat Pay with a Chinese bank-linked card or use the tourist foreign card option.
Pengshui food highlight
Miao sour fish soup (酸鱼汤) is a genuine regional speciality — very different to Chongqing's spice focus. Order it for the family; it's a crowd-pleaser even for those who fear spice.
Pengshui return train
Book the Pengshui → Chongqing North train in advance on Trip.com or 12306.cn. Peak demand in summer. Early morning trains (7:30–8am) have more availability.
Charter car for Wulong/Pengshui
For 4 people with luggage, a private charter van is far more practical than buses. Many guesthouses and hotels in Chongqing can arrange this. Budget ¥900–1,200/day all-in.
Zoo — morning is key
Pandas are most active at feeding time between 9–11am. By afternoon they're sleeping. The zoo is large — factor 3+ hours and wear light shoes.
June rain strategy
Schedule outdoor attractions (hikes, ropeway, Chaotianmen) for mornings. Rain usually hits in the afternoon. Use rainy windows for the museum, aquarium, and Starlight 68 mall.
Visa first. Australians need a Chinese L-visa. Apply at the Chinese Consulate-General in Brisbane or Sydney. Allow 4–6 weeks minimum. Verify current requirements — Australia has not yet been added to China's visa-free list.
