The Thesis
Warsh's plan: cut short rates, steepen the yield curve, remove SLR constraints so banks absorb Treasury supply. Net effect: covert QE through the banking system, not the Fed's balance sheet. Prerequisite: Iran deal to reopen Hormuz, relieve oil inflation, and give Warsh room to cut. Supercycle thesis: we are 5-6 years into a 14-22 year rotation from financial assets to real assets — gold, silver, commodities — driven by deglobalisation, record debt, and structural dollar debasement. Stocks go up in dollar terms. Hard assets go up more.
